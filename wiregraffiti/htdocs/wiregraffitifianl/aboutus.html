Wire Graffiti, a four piece rock band from Oakland, CA, has been battering sound systems and frenzying fans for 15 years. Powered by vocalist/guitarist Katherine Sawyer and guitarist Stev Ohanis this crowd-rousing band is driven full force by drummer Rob Tucker and bassist Rafa Corral.

Wire Graffiti have always played what they want, and their most current self-titled effort is no exception. With Katherine and Stev's combined influences the songs are given plenty of juice and plugged into a user-friendly rock album that spotlights what Wire Graffiti is all about, one big rebellious party.

Songwriter's Katherine Sawyer and Stev Ohanis met 18 years ago over a game of pool at Oakland's George and Walt's and haven't looked back. After hitting the clubs in May of 1997 Wire Graffiti's first CD, Poetic Dictionary, a mostly folk-based album written primarily by Katherine and co-written, recorded and produced by Stev, was featured on KFOG's Local Anesthetic. With Stev's quirky rock style rubbing off on Katherine their sound began to fuse while playing live with band members Tim Sanchez on bass and drummer DC Carter.

Drummer Derek DeFields stepped in for Wire Graffiti's second recording, Hitting Traffic, which was released in 2000. Rob Tucker came in on drums for the live shows and Hitting Traffic established the band in the underground club scene, with an ever growing fan base, college radio exposure and local television spots including Bay TV's nationally broadcast 'The Show'.

With Tim Sanchez still on bass and Rob Tucker now on drums, the band released their third full length CD, Peculiar Love, on Valentine¹s Day 2003. Taking the material in a decidely quirky and complicated direction they brought in producer Scrote (Casino Royale, Baby Snufkin) This CD features guest artists Dave Palmer (Seal, Fiona Apple, Brian Eno) on keyboards, horn players Mike and Travis Lafferty, and Angelique X (Venus Bleeding, Charm School Dropouts, T&A) lending her seasoned chops to the vocal mix.
